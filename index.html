Muhammad Ali

Muhammad Ali (1942-2016) was an American former heavyweight champion boxer and one of the greatest sporting figures of the 20th century. An Olympic gold medalist and the first fighter to capture the heavyweight title three times, Ali won 56 times in his 21-year professional career.
Country: United State America
Nickname: The Greatest
Division: Light heavyweight / Heavyweight
Record: 56-5 37KO
Pro Debut: October 29 1960

Mike Tyson

He reigned as the undisputed world heavyweight champion from 1987 to 1990. Tyson won his first 19 professional fights by knockout, 12 of them in the first round. Claiming his first belt at 20 years, four months, and 22 days old, Tyson holds the record as the youngest boxer ever to win a heavyweight title.He was the first heavyweight boxer to simultaneously hold the WBA, WBC and IBF titles, as well as the only heavyweight to unify them in succession.
Country: United State America
Nickname: Iron Mike
Division: Heavyweight
Record: 50-6-2NC 44KO
Pro Debut: March 6 1985

Manny Pacquiao

Manny Pacquiao is a Filipino former professional boxer who competed from 1995 to 2021. Regarded by many boxing historians as one of the greatest professional boxers of all time, Pacquiao is the only boxer in history to win twelve major world titles in eight different weight divisions. He is also the first boxer in history to win the lineal championship in five different weight divisions, as well as being the first boxer in history to win major world titles in four of the original eight weight divisions of boxing, also known as the "glamour divisions": flyweight, featherweight, lightweight and welterweight.
Country: Philipines
Nickname: Pacman
Division: Lightweight
Record: 62-8-2 39KO
Pro Debut: 29 October 1960

About Boxing

What is Boxing?

Boxing is a sport taht involves strategically punching an opponent while defending yourself from their return punches. Some amateurs practice boxing as a way to stay in shape. Boxing match involves two figthers in a ring surrounded by thick ropes. They wear protective guard, padded gloves and mouth guard, and thier figth is monitored by a referee. Rules of boxing include not hitting below the waist, from behind, or when an opponent is on the ground. The objective, however is to knock the other person out or hit them so hard that they can't get up within ten seconds.

Different between Professional and Amateur Boxing

Professional

Pay Check- Professional boxers box for money, and it is thier job. They get paid large fees for fights since it is thier livelilhood.

Entertaiment- Professional boxing is all about entertaining the crowd, which is where the revenue for paying the fgthers comes from.

Gloves- In professional fights, all competitors up to welterweight will use an 8 oz glove. Everyone in higher weight clases should use the 10 oz glove.

Bouts- Professional boxing bouts are usually fought over six to ten rounds depending upon the experience of the boxers, championship is twelve rounds.

Protective Wear- Professional boxers do not wear protective headgear. Some important basics include boxing gloves, hand wraps or gauze, a mouth guard, boxing shoes and groin guard.

Amateur

Pay Check- Amateur boxers do not box full time but rather as a sport. Amateur figthers earnings are usually low, and they have a ceiling of earnings to retain amateur status

Entertaiment- Amateur boxing is all about the sport aspect of boxing rather than crowd entertaining.

Gloves- Amateurs wear larger gloves as a precaution to prevent younger bodies from suffering trauma.

Bouts- Amateur boxing bouts are a maximum of 3 rounds, with each round being 3 minutes in duration.

Protective Wear- Amateur boxers wear protective headgear to help protect boxers from knockouts and the associated injuries. Some important basics include boxing gloves, hand wraps or gauze, a mouth guard, boxing shoes, groin guard, and clothes you're comfortable boxing in.
